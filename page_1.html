UMSI Academic Advising

Welcome! This page contains information about academic advising, academic planning, and student support resources for students in the Bachelor of Science in Information (BSI) Program.

Overview

Academic advising is a collaborative process where students work with a qualified UMSI Academic Advisor to envision, implement, and reflect on their academic plans. Students share their goals, challenges, and questions, while the advisor provides expertise on university and program requirements, policies, and procedures. Together, they create an academic plan that supports the student's personal and professional aspirations.

Tips For Getting The Most Out Of Advising

As academic advisors, we are here for you and want to see you get the most out of your experience in the BSI program. We offer several ways to connect with us, which you can see on our Connecting With An Advisor page.

Find the Right Service for You

  • Scheduled 1:1 Appointments: These 15- or 30-minute meetings must be scheduled at least 24 hours in advance and are ideal for addressing unique or complex questions or concerns. In some cases, your advisor may recommend or require you to schedule an appointment if a question raised via email or during drop-in advising needs more time to address.
  • Drop-in 1:1 Advising Appointments: Offered weekly without pre-registration, drop-in appointments allow students to meet with any available advisor on a first-come, first-served basis. These sessions are best suited for time-sensitive questions that can be resolved in 10 minutes or less.
  • Email Communication: Email provides an effective way to connect with the advising team asynchronously, especially for students managing busy schedules. It also creates a written record of the conversation for future reference. The advising team aims to respond to student emails within 24 business hours (Monday–Friday, 9 AM–5 PM, excluding holidays), though response times may vary.

Mark Down the Deadlines

  • Students should stay informed about key dates, such as semester start and end dates, add/drop deadlines, and any other deadlines that may impact their experience, like those related to financial aid. For official university deadlines, refer to the UM Registrar Calendar.
  • Plan ahead by scheduling advising appointments well before these deadlines. It's a good idea to allow extra time in case follow-up steps are needed after your appointment.
  • If reaching out via email, do so several days before any upcoming deadlines.
  • Whenever possible, connect with your advisor proactively—before an issue arises or becomes urgent. While advisors are here to assist during challenges, they must work within the university's guidelines, procedures, and policies.

Preparing for Advising Appointments

Making the most of your advising appointment begins with proper preparation. Here are some steps to help you get ready:

  1. Know Your Goals: Identify what you want to achieve during the meeting. Whether it's planning your course schedule, discussing career goals, or addressing a specific challenge, having a clear purpose will help you and your advisor stay focused.
  2. Review Relevant Information: Check your degree requirements, course options, and any emails or resources related to your questions. Familiarize yourself with key dates and deadlines, such as add/drop deadlines or graduation requirements, to guide the discussion.
  3. Prepare Questions and Topics: Write down any specific questions, concerns, or topics you'd like to cover. This ensures you won't forget anything important during the meeting.
  4. Bring Necessary Materials: Have any relevant documents, such as your unofficial transcript, degree audit, or a draft course schedule, ready to share.
  5. Be Open and Honest: Share your goals, challenges, or uncertainties openly. The more your advisor understands your situation, the better they can guide you.
  6. Follow Up and Take Action: After your appointment, review any notes or action items you discussed. Complete any next steps promptly, such as submitting forms, adjusting your schedule, or researching opportunities.

Preparing for your advising appointment not only helps you get the answers you need but also ensures a more productive and efficient conversation.
